About

who · what · why

I'm an engineer who likes the hands-on end of hardware. At VINSE — Vanderbilt's nanofabrication facility — I run CVD graphene growth and transfer, e-beam lithography, ALD, and RIE in a Class‑100 cleanroom, support 20+ researchers, and supervise a team of four. I rebuilt our CVD reactor and cut its leak rate roughly tenfold, which is the project I'm proudest of because it now sits under everyone else's work.

Off the bench I build software for the same world: Ardic, an AI copilot for cleanroom operations, and a chip I carried from Verilog to fabricated silicon. The throughline is the same — get close to the physical process, then make it more reliable and more legible. I'm aiming for hands-on fab and process work, and I learn fastest by shipping.
